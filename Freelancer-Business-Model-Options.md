# Tip Share Pro: Freelancer Business Model Options

**For:** Development Agreement with Tom LaChaussee
**Date:** December 2025
**Document Type:** Business Model Analysis & Recommendations

---

## Your Current Situation

You've already quoted Tom:
- **25 hours × €230/hour = €5,750** (~$6,100 USD) for the full web application
- You've built a sophisticated mockup (unpaid, as proof of concept)
- Tom has indicated he sees "potential for ongoing income" - he's thinking SaaS

**Key constraint:** You cannot increase the €5,750 development quote without damaging trust.

**Opportunity:** You CAN add ongoing revenue participation as a separate component - this is standard for SaaS products and aligns with Tom's own business model.

---

## Executive Summary

| Option | Development Fee | Ongoing Revenue | 3-Year Total (200 restaurants) |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|-------------------------------|
| A: Quote + Revenue Share | €5,750 | €5/location/month | ~€17,750 |
| B: Discount for Higher Share | €4,000 | €10/location/month | ~€28,000 |
| C: Quote Only (No Ongoing) | €5,750 | €0 | €5,750 |

**Recommendation:** Option A - Honor your quote, add modest revenue participation

---

## Option A: Honor Quote + Add Revenue Share (RECOMMENDED)

### Structure
```
Development Fee: €5,750 (as quoted)
Ongoing: €5 per active restaurant location per month
```

### Why This Works

1. **You keep your word** - The €5,750 quote stands, trust intact
2. **It's fair** - You're building the engine that generates his revenue
3. **Tom already thinks SaaS** - He mentioned "ongoing income" himself
4. **Small ask** - €5/location is ~3-5% of what he'll charge restaurants
5. **Aligned incentives** - You're motivated to help the product succeed

### Financial Projection

| Year | Restaurants | Your Monthly | Your Annual | Cumulative |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Year 0 | - | - | €5,750 (dev) | €5,750 |
| Year 1 | 25 avg | €125 | €1,500 | €7,250 |
| Year 2 | 75 avg | €375 | €4,500 | €11,750 |
| Year 3 | 150 avg | €750 | €9,000 | €20,750 |

**3-Year Total: ~€20,750** (vs €5,750 one-time)

### How to Present to Tom

> "Hi Tom,
>
> The mockup is ready and I'm excited to move forward. I'm honoring our discussed estimate of **€5,750** for the full web application development.
>
> Since this will be a subscription product generating recurring revenue for you, I'd like to propose a small ongoing participation: **€5 per active restaurant location per month**. This keeps me invested in the product's long-term success - I'll be motivated to maintain it, fix issues quickly, and help you improve it over time.
>
> At 50 restaurants, that's €250/month to me. At 200 restaurants, €1,000/month. It scales with your success, and it's a small fraction of what you'll charge each restaurant (likely €39-149/month based on the market).
>
> Let me know your thoughts - happy to discuss."

### If Tom Pushes Back

| His Objection | Your Response |
|---------------|---------------|
| "I want to own it outright" | "I understand. The €5,750 gives you full ownership. The €5/location is optional but keeps me engaged for ongoing support and improvements." |
| "That adds up over time" | "True, but it's aligned with your success. If you have 100 restaurants paying €79/month, you're making €7,900/month. My €500 is 6%." |
| "Can we cap it?" | "Sure - how about capped at €1,000/month? That protects you at scale." |
| "Let me think about it" | "Of course. We can also start without it and revisit after launch if you prefer." |

## Option B: Discount Development for Higher Ongoing Share

### Structure
```
Development Fee: €4,000 (reduced from €5,750)
Ongoing: €10 per active restaurant location per month
```

### When to Use This

Only offer this if:
- Tom hesitates on Option A
- Tom has cash flow concerns
- You're very confident in the product's success

### Financial Projection

| Year | Restaurants | Your Monthly | Your Annual | Cumulative |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Year 0 | - | - | €4,000 (dev) | €4,000 |
| Year 1 | 25 avg | €250 | €3,000 | €7,000 |
| Year 2 | 75 avg | €750 | €9,000 | €16,000 |
| Year 3 | 150 avg | €1,500 | €18,000 | €34,000 |

**3-Year Total: ~€34,000** (higher upside, more risk)

### How to Present

> "If the upfront cost is a concern, I could reduce it to €4,000 in exchange for €10 per location monthly. Lower barrier to start, and we both benefit as the product grows."

## Option C: Stick to Quote Only (NOT RECOMMENDED)

### Structure
```
Development Fee: €5,750 (as quoted)
Ongoing: Nothing
```

### Why This is Suboptimal

- You're building a SaaS product - recurring revenue is the whole point
- Tom will potentially make €100K+/year from this; you get €5,750 total
- No incentive for you to provide ongoing support
- No long-term relationship
- You're leaving significant money on the table

### When This Makes Sense

Only if:
- Tom absolutely refuses any ongoing participation
- You want a clean break and no ongoing obligations
- You don't believe the product will succeed

### The Math That Should Bother You

If Tom gets 200 restaurants paying €79/month average:
- **Tom's annual revenue:** €189,600
- **Your total compensation:** €5,750 (3% of ONE year's revenue)

## The Psychology of Asking

### Why You're Hesitant

You might be thinking:
- "I already quoted €5,750, I can't ask for more"
- "He might say no and I'll lose the project"
- "I don't want to seem greedy"

### Why You Shouldn't Be

1. **You're not changing the quote** - €5,750 for development stands
2. **Revenue share is standard** - It's how SaaS partnerships work
3. **Tom benefits too** - You stay invested in his success
4. **It's a small ask** - €5/location is nothing compared to his revenue
5. **He can say no** - And you still do the project at €5,750

### The Worst That Happens

Tom says "No, I just want to pay the €5,750."

You say "No problem, let's proceed."

You're exactly where you started. No downside.

## Contract Terms for Option A

```
TIP SHARE PRO - DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT

1. DEVELOPMENT

   1.1 Scope: Full web application as demonstrated in mockup

   1.2 Fee: €5,750 EUR
       - 50% (€2,875) due upon signing
       - 50% (€2,875) due upon deployment

   1.3 Timeline: 4-6 weeks from signing

   1.4 Included: 60 days of bug fixes post-launch

2. ONGOING PARTICIPATION

   2.1 Fee: €5.00 EUR per active restaurant location per month

   2.2 "Active Location" = Any location with user login during billing month

   2.3 Payment: Monthly, due within 15 days of invoice

   2.4 Reporting: Client provides location count by 5th of each month

   2.5 Cap: €1,500/month maximum (optional, if Tom wants ceiling)

3. TERM

   3.1 Ongoing fees begin 30 days after first paying customer

   3.2 Agreement runs for 3 years, auto-renews annually

   3.3 Either party may terminate with 90 days notice

   3.4 Buyout: Client may end ongoing fees by paying 24x monthly average

4. OWNERSHIP

   4.1 Client owns Tip Share Pro product, brand, and customer relationships

   4.2 Developer may showcase in portfolio with permission

```

## What If Tom Wants a Maintenance Agreement Instead?

If Tom prefers a fixed monthly fee over per-location:

```
Alternative: Fixed Maintenance Retainer

Monthly Fee: €300-500/month

Includes:
- Hosting management
- Security updates
- Bug fixes (up to 4 hours/month)
- Minor improvements
- Email support

NOT included (billed hourly):
- New features
- Major changes
- POS integrations
```

This gives you predictable income (~€3,600-6,000/year) without tying to location count. Less upside, but guaranteed.

## My Recommendation

**Go with Option A:**

1. **Send Tom the mockup** with enthusiasm
2. **Confirm the €5,750 development fee** - show you're honoring your word
3. **Propose €5/location/month** as aligned partnership
4. **Be ready to negotiate** down to €3/location or add a cap
5. **Accept gracefully** if he declines - do the project anyway

You've built something impressive. The mockup demonstrates real value. Tom is clearly interested. You have leverage to ask for ongoing participation - use it respectfully.

## Scripts for Common Scenarios

### If Tom Says Yes Immediately
> "Great! I'll draft a simple agreement covering both the development fee and the ongoing participation. Should have it to you by [date]."

### If Tom Wants to Negotiate
> "I'm flexible on the exact number. What feels fair to you? I'm mainly looking for something that keeps me invested in making this successful for you."

### If Tom Says No to Ongoing
> "No problem at all - I understand wanting to keep it simple. Let's proceed with the €5,750 for development. I'm excited to build this for you."

### If Tom Ghosts on the Ongoing Discussion
Follow up once, then proceed with development-only if needed. Don't let the revenue discussion kill the deal.

---

## Final Thought

**You're not being greedy. You're being professional.**

Tom is building a business that could generate €100K+/year from software you're creating. Asking for 3-5% of that through a small per-location fee is reasonable, fair, and standard in this industry.

The worst case: he says no, and you build it for €5,750.
The best case: you create a passive income stream worth €10K+/year.

**Ask.**
